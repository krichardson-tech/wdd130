Client Report

How did you decide what content and images to use for your site plan?

I really like to read and decided to make my website book themed. When I decided on the them, I them thought about what I could do with books. I wanted to encourage people to read and get into books more which is why I eventually decided on what I did. Badges and challenges to engourage more reading, quotes to help you enjoy your book more, and book searching and sharing to discover and bond over more books.

How did you communicate with your developer? Was there any issues with communication?

I mostly communicated with my developer in person, though we did exchange numbers so we texted a bit as well as our form of long-distance communication. Sometimes it was hard to get a hold of each other as one of us would text while the other was busy or unavailable, but we never really had to wait an exessive amount of time for the other to reply, so it wasn't too bad.

Did your developer meet your expectations for your site?

Mostly, yes. I didn't know exactly how I wanted the colors and they delived beautifully. I loved how everything was decorated and formatted. The only thing they couldn't deliver was the type of gallery I wanted was different then the one they provided but the one I wanted was more then they could do at their level right now. They already had to do some Java for this gallery which was above the "pay grade" for this assignment, but I do really like the gallery that they made. Other than that minor thing, I love it!

What will you do differently next time you hire a developer to do a project for you?

I think that I'll probably specify the footer better. I didn't really offer much for the footer and so I ended up having to go back and forth to make sure that it was something I liked. Basically, I just need to be specific on what I want and what they can play around with.

General Experience and Reflections

What was the most challenging part of this project for you? Were you able to overcome it?

I think the hardest part was when my CSS wouldn't do what I was trying to make it do. Those unexpected errors were tricky to figure out and fix, making them very annoying and frustrating. Like when I made my logo a link to the home page. It messed up the positioning in the nav so I had to make new code and alter existing code, just so it went make to the position I had and liked from before I turned the logo into a link.

What did you enjoy most about this project?

When the CSS wasn't giving me annoying errors, I actually really enjoyed putting everything together, HTML-wise, and designing it in CSS was fun too, even if it took a bit to find something I liked that made good use of the color scheme.

What would you do differently if you could do the project again?

I'd probably try to make it more like an actual website. I like it, it feels like somthing I made, but it doesn't really give off official website vibes. Maybe I'd also have the drop-shadow border the images instead of just being on two sides. I don't really know what I'd change without adding more content than what the client provided and possibly changing their wireframe, but that would make it less like their vision.

Do you feel more confident in working on real-world projects after this experience? Explain.

I actually do. I was worried about making all this from scratch but when I realized that they would provide things (color scheme, images, wireframes, content, and any preferences), it made me feel so much better. I prefer being told what they want and while I'm creative, I do my best creativity when I have somthing to go off of.

I was also worried about if I was actually able to remember and apply everything I learned when I needed it. We progressed through everything so quickly that I wasn't sure how well I actually retained it all. This helped me feel more confident in my skills and knowledge.
